Operating System.

Week 1

Uncover what you've really been chasing. Approval. Power. Control. Comfort. Shift from seeking these in the world to finding them in Christ. This reframes your entire identity.

Define the story you want to live and the legacy you want to leave. When you know what matters at the end of your life, you gain clarity for how to live today.

Map out your 10-Year Goals, 5-Year Projects, and 1-Year Priorities. Stop guessing. Start acting with purpose and precision.

You don't rise to the level of your goals. you fall to the level of your systems. Design the rhythms that make consistency automatic.

Start each day grounded in purpose, the Word, and gratitude. Stay anchored in who you are, not what the world demands.

Week 9

Track daily deposits across faith, family, fitness, finance, and freedom. Real transformation comes from small, consistent deposits over time.

Meet Matt

About Matt Chenard

Matt's story is one of crisis, conviction, and calling.

In 2012, he co-founded a gym in a 400-square-foot garage with nothing but a passion for fitness and an idea. Over the next seven years, he scaled that gym into an almost seven-figure business in a town of 20,000 people. By 2019, everything looked perfect on paper.

But behind the scenes, Matt was burning out.

He was doing too much. He didn't have systems. He wasn't pursuing the Lord first. He was operating from a place of approval—chasing success, chasing achievement, chasing the next milestone. His identity was wrapped up in what he accomplished, not in who he was in Christ.

Then the lockdowns hit.

Five lockdowns. $1.2 million in losses. Everything he'd built was on the verge of collapse. It was the crescendo of a system that had been breaking down for years.

That's when everything shifted.

Matt realized he'd been operating from striving, not stewardship. From approval, not identity in Christ. He made a radical decision to rebuild—not just his business, but his life.

He would operate from stewardship, not striving. From identity in Christ, not approval from others. From being present as a husband and father, not just being successful as an entrepreneur.

He committed to showing up with consistency and conviction every single day. Not because the circumstances were perfect, but because his identity was rooted in something deeper.

Over the next few years, Matt developed the Mission 5 Framework—a system built on stewardship, identity in Christ, and unbreakable discipline. It's the same system he uses to show up as a husband, father, and man of faith every single day.

He's now coached over 100 Christian entrepreneurs through this framework. The gap between where they are and where they want to be doesn't close by working harder. It closes when you shift from striving for approval to operating from stewardship. When you build from identity in Christ, not achievement.

That's what MOAM was built to do.
